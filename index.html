Principal Component Pursuit By Alternating Directions

Implemented Robust PCA using Alternating Direction Method and applied it to denoise the Yale face dataset

MATLAB, Python

Semester: 4

Team size: 4

Date: July 2022

Grade: A+

In this project, we explored the limitations of traditional Principal Component Analysis (PCA) and implemented Robust Principal Component Analysis (RPCA) to overcome these limitations:

  • PCA Limitations: Demonstrated how standard PCA is not robust to outliers, affecting its performance in real-world scenarios with noisy data.
  • RPCA Implementation: Implemented RPCA by framing it as an Alternating Direction Method (ADM) problem, providing a more robust approach to dimensionality reduction.
  • Yale Face Dataset Application: Applied our RPCA implementation to the Yale face dataset, showcasing its effectiveness in image processing tasks.
  • Noise Introduction and Denoising: Introduced various types of noise to the dataset, including:
    • Random patches
    • Salt and pepper noise
    • Other artificial distortions
  • Comparative Analysis: Demonstrated the superior performance of RPCA in denoising these corrupted images compared to traditional PCA.
  • Mathematical Foundation: Derived and implemented the mathematical foundations of RPCA and the Alternating Direction Method.
This project deepened our understanding of robust dimensionality reduction techniques and their practical applications in image processing and denoising. It showcased the power of RPCA in handling real-world data with outliers and corruption, providing valuable insights into advanced data analysis methods.

Reinforcement Learning

Derived and Implemented 6 RL algorithms from scratch in Python and simulated with GYM environments

Python, GYM

Semester: 3

Team size: 4

Date: Jan 2022

Grade: A+

In this comprehensive project, we explored and implemented a range of reinforcement learning algorithms, from classic to cutting-edge:

  • Q-learning: Implemented this foundational value-based algorithm, understanding the concept of off-policy learning and the balance between exploration and exploitation.
  • SARSA (State-Action-Reward-State-Action): Developed this on-policy TD control algorithm, comparing its performance with Q-learning in various environments.
  • DQN (Deep Q-Network): Extended Q-learning to deep learning, implementing experience replay and fixed Q-targets to stabilize training in complex environments.
  • REINFORCE with baseline: Implemented this policy gradient method, incorporating a baseline to reduce variance and improve learning stability.
  • PPO (Proximal Policy Optimization): Implemented this state-of-the-art policy optimization algorithm, known for its sample efficiency and reliability.
  • DDPG (Deep Deterministic Policy Gradient): Developed this actor-critic algorithm for continuous action spaces, combining insights from DQN and deterministic policy gradients.
For each algorithm, we:
  • Derived the mathematical foundations and update rules
  • Implemented the algorithm from scratch in Python
  • Tested and benchmarked performance on various OpenAI Gym environments
  • Analyzed learning curves, policy behavior, and algorithm trade-offs
This project deepened our understanding of RL fundamentals, policy and value-based methods, and advanced techniques in deep reinforcement learning, providing hands-on experience with algorithms at the forefront of AI research.
